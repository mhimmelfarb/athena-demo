Refine Customer Segmentation to Improve Retention

üéØ Hypothesis: Your Retention Problem May Be a Segmentation Problem

Your 89% gross retention rate is an average across all customer segments. This likely masks significant variance:

Scenario A: All segments churn at ~11% for similar reasons (onboarding issues, poor value realization, support gaps) ‚Üí This is an execution problem. Fix: Invest in CS enablement, onboarding, value communication.

Scenario B: One segment (e.g., construction companies 20-100 employees) has 95% retention, but another segment (e.g., delivery companies <20 employees) has 75% retention ‚Üí This is a segmentation problem. Fix: Refine your ICP, deprioritize low-retention segments, double down on high-retention segments.

Key insight: If Scenario B is true, you're spending the same CAC to acquire customers with wildly different LTVs. At a 2:1 LTV:CAC ratio (already below the 4.5-5√ó benchmark), you cannot afford to acquire low-retention customers.

Recommendation: Before investing in demand generation, run a retention segmentation analysis in Salesforce. Identify which customer cohorts have the best retention, highest LTV, and easiest sales cycles. Then refine your ICP and messaging to attract more of those customers.
