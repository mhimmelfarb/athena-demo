Competitive Battle Cards
VS. VERACODE
Platform Play - When you'll encounter them: Enterprise deals wanting "one vendor for everything"
Their Strengths (Acknowledge Honestly)
  • Brand recognition: 6.9% market share vs. your 0.3% (PeerSpot data)
  • Platform breadth: SAST, DAST, SCA across web, mobile, API in single vendor
  • Enterprise maturity: Established procurement relationships, known quantity for risk-averse buyers
  • Integrated workflow: Single dashboard for all application security testing
Where You Win
  • Mobile depth: Binary-level analysis they can't match, MARI for third-party app vetting (unique capability)
  • Ease of use: G2 buyers rate you 4.6 vs. their 4.2 on usability
  • Time to value: 2 weeks to operational vs. their 4-6 weeks (customer feedback)
  • False positive rate: 40-50% fewer false positives for mobile-specific vulnerabilities
  • Support quality: Dedicated mobile security expertise vs. generalist platform support
Win Scenarios
You win when:
  • Mobile apps are mission-critical revenue drivers (fintech, healthcare, retail)
  • Buyer values depth over breadth (security-first vs. platform consolidation mindset)
  • Fast implementation is priority (quarterly security reviews vs. annual contracts)
  • Third-party app vetting is required (supply chain risk concerns)
They win when:
  • Buyer wants web + mobile + API in single vendor (platform consolidation)
  • Existing Veracode relationship for web security (expansion vs. new vendor)
  • Procurement strongly prefers single-vendor solutions
Trap-Setting Discovery Question
"Walk me through your mobile app strategy. If mobile apps drive 40%+ of your revenue or customer engagement, are you comfortable with a vendor whose mobile capability is an add-on to their web platform rather than their core competency? What happens when a mobile-specific vulnerability like insecure data storage or binary tampering gets missed because the tool was optimized for web?"
Objection Handlers
Objection: "We want one vendor for all application security"
Response: "I understand the appeal of consolidation. Let me ask: would you use the same tool to test a Boeing 747 and a Formula 1 car? Both are vehicles, but they require specialized expertise. Mobile apps have unique attack surfaces—binary tampering, insecure data storage, jailbreak detection—that platform tools often miss or flag incorrectly. The question is: does mobile matter enough to your business to warrant best-in-class depth?"
VS. APPKNOX
Mobile Specialist, Lower Price Point - When you'll encounter them: Price-sensitive mid-market deals
Their angle: "We're mobile-only like NowSecure but at 30-40% lower price"

Your counter: You differentiate on MARI (they don't have third-party app intelligence), enterprise-grade support, and compliance depth (OWASP MASVS certified testing). Win scenario: Regulated industries (fintech, healthcare) where compliance evidence is non-negotiable.

Trap question: "Are you subject to PCI-DSS, HIPAA, or similar compliance requirements that require documented OWASP MASVS testing? How do you currently prove compliance to auditors?"
VS. CHECKMARX
SAST Platform with Mobile Add-On
Similar to Veracode positioning. Win on mobile depth and MARI. They win on developer-first workflow and code-level remediation guidance.
VS. "DO NOTHING"
Status Quo / Manual Testing
Their thinking: "We'll just do manual pen testing annually"
Your counter: Manual testing is point-in-time (app changes weekly). Automated continuous testing catches vulnerabilities before production. ROI: $400K avg breach cost vs. $45K/year prevention.
Trap question: "How many times did your mobile app get updated last quarter? If you're shipping weekly but testing annually, what's your exposure window?"
