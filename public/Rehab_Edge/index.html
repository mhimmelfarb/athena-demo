1
ICP Clarity: Pick ONE Market to Dominate

What We Observed

  • Website lists 8 different practitioner types as target customers
  • Founder credentials and podcast content are PT-focused
  • Three homepage questions reference PT-specific pain points (training camps, sponsorships, provider referrals)
  • EquityNet funding goal shows early-stage ($25K raised of $500K goal)

Strategic Direction

  • Consider narrowing to independent PT/OT clinics ($500K-$2M annual revenue, 1-3 locations) as beachhead market
  • Different verticals have fundamentally different referral models - psychologists get referrals differently than PT clinics
  • VCs will ask "Who's your ICP?" - saying "all healthcare providers with referrals" signals you haven't found product-market fit yet
  • A focused ICP enables: repeatable sales motion, specific messaging, relevant case studies, and defensible positioning
2
Value Articulation: What's the Dollar Impact?

What We Observed

  • Hero headline asks a curiosity question ("Do You Know Where Your Referrals Are Coming From?") without establishing urgency
  • Features section lists "Real-time marketing ROI insights" and "AI-enabled forecasting" but doesn't connect to business outcomes
  • Interactive dashboard is well-executed, but lacks outcome context (e.g., "This clinic cut $2K/month waste")
  • No visible customer testimonials quantifying ROI or results

Strategic Direction

  • Consider reframing from curiosity ("Do you know?") to urgency ("You're spending $5K-$10K/month on marketing - which parts fill your schedule?")
  • Connect features to outcomes: "Real-time ROI insights" ‚Üí "Stop wasting 40% of marketing budget on channels that don't convert"
  • Add economic context to examples: "Most clinics waste $50K-$150K annually" or similar anchoring
  • Beta customers likely have early insights - consider even qualitative early wins to demonstrate value
3
Pricing Visibility: Build Confidence Through Transparency

What We Observed

  • No pricing visible on website - appears to be "contact us" model
  • Beta/pilot language suggests you're still validating pricing
  • No pricing tiers, seat-based models, or usage indicators
  • For fundraising: VCs will ask about ACV, segmentation strategy, and pricing confidence

Strategic Direction

  • Consider publishing pricing tiers once you have validation from beta customers (e.g., Basic $199/mo, Professional $499/mo, Enterprise "Call us")
  • Pricing visibility signals market confidence and helps qualify leads
  • Early-stage companies often fear losing deals by showing pricing, but transparent pricing actually improves conversion by pre-qualifying buyers
  • For investor conversations: be prepared to discuss pricing strategy, ACV targets, and what drives expansion
